# My Case Study Repository 📚
This repository contains a curated set of product management case studies I built while practicing problem framing, product thinking, market analysis, prioritization, and roadmap design.

Each case study is written in Markdown and is structured to walk through the product from multiple angles: business context, user problems, metrics, competitive landscape, opportunity identification, and feature recommendations.

## What You’ll Find Here ✨

- Product teardowns for consumer internet and marketplace businesses
- Structured PM frameworks such as personas, funnels, North Star metrics, AARRR, RICE, SWOT, and PRDs
- Thoughtful feature proposals and roadmap exercises grounded in real product challenges
- A growing archive of case studies that can be used for interview prep, portfolio building, or self-study

## Case Studies 🗂️

- [Airbnb Case Study](Case_Studies/AirBnb_caseStudy.md) - Focuses on the post-booking experience gap: trust, coordination, and what happens after the reservation is made.
- [Amazon Case Study](Case_Studies/Amazon_caseStudy.md) - Explores how selection can become a liability when it creates friction, overload, and weak decision support.
- [Blinkit Case Study](Case_Studies/Blinkit_caseStudy.md) - Examines density economics in quick commerce and the operational tradeoffs behind 10-minute delivery.
- [LinkedIn Case Study](Case_Studies/LinkedIn_caseStudy.md) - Looks at disintermediation risk and how a platform keeps value creation inside the network.
- [Swiggy Case Study](Case_Studies/Swiggy_caseStudy.md) - Analyzes Swiggy's two-speed P&L, where profitable food delivery funds a capital-hungry quick-commerce bet.

## How To Use 🚀

1. Open any case study in the `Case_Studies/` folder.
2. Read the problem framing and user insights first.
3. Review the metrics, prioritization, and roadmap sections to understand the product thinking.
4. Use the documents as reference material for PM interview prep or your own case-study practice.

## Notes

- The case studies are written as learning artifacts, so some assumptions are explicitly labeled where public data is unavailable.
- The content is intended to demonstrate product thinking and structured analysis rather than serve as official company documentation.

## Purpose 🎯

My goal with this repository is to keep building a strong portfolio of product management work that shows how I think about users, businesses, and execution tradeoffs.
